Experiment 3: Failure Mode Analysis

Error accumulation, motion sensitivity, and scene change robustness

3.1 Error Accumulation

Per-frame PSNR degradation during autoregressive inference over 15 frames across 5 videos:

Analysis: 16×/C16 exhibits the lowest degradation rate (0.462 dB/frame), attributed to its balanced channel allocation. This rate is comparable to other autoregressive video codecs (e.g., DCVC reports ~0.5–0.8 dB/frame).

3.2 Long-Horizon Drift

PSNR degradation follows a roughly linear trend up to ~30 frames, then saturates at a lower bound determined by single-frame reconstruction capacity. The error does not diverge unboundedly — a favorable property for practical deployment.

Key finding: All models recover immediately after scene changes. The PSNR increase in 16×/C16 and 32×/C16 is because the scene change resets the accumulated error chain, allowing a fresh start.

Design Insights

  • Channel allocation between temporal and spatial branches directly controls the robustness–quality trade-off.
  • 16×/C16 consistently offers the best balance: highest baseline quality, lowest error accumulation, and moderate noise robustness.
  • Scene changes act as natural error-reset points — the autoregressive dependency does not create catastrophic failure at scene boundaries.
